PETS · safety

🛡️ PawShield #1 overall

Log your pet’s foods, treats & meds. PawShield watches FDA recalls + the federal animal adverse-event corpus and tells you, with citations, which of your products carry elevated risk for a pet like yours.

Data-model centerpiece: a per-cohort statistical-lift materialized view (this ingredient’s serious-reaction rate for your species/breed/age vs baseline) joined to pgvector ingredient-linkage that matches messy product strings (“chicken jerky treats” ≈ a recalled SKU).
Target audience
Owners of senior pets, chronic-meds pets, and anxious “researcher” owners. US pet industry ≈ $150B/yr; ≈ 90M pet-owning households.

STOCKS · research

📊 Rhyme #2 · highest “wow”

Pick a ticker’s latest earnings call → Rhyme finds the most semantically similar past calls market-wide, then shows what the stock, insiders & shorts did in the next 90 days. “History rhymes.”

Data-model centerpiece: one query traverses a vector ANN over transcript segments → partitioned time-series forward-returns → insider events. Plus an “evasiveness” score = cosine distance between each analyst question and management’s answer (2026 research; no retail product ships it).

Market & competition

Where the open lane is

📊 Stocks — crowded, except one lane

13F/insider/Congress trackers are saturated (WhaleWisdom, Fintel, Quiver, Capitol Trades, and 2026’s “Meridian” already selling “signal convergence”). AI trade-journaling/coaching is table stakes (TradeZella, TraderSync). The open lane: earnings-call semantics + historical analogues — today only in enterprise tools (AlphaSense, Hebbia, Serafis) & academia. Rhyme owns the retail version. (This is why your earlier “Prism” divergence idea ranked last — it’s the crowded lane.)

🐾 Pets — chatbots everywhere, data ignored

Saturated around AI-vet-triage chat (Voyage AI Vet, PetWellness AI) and health-record vaults (PetDesk, VitusVet) — thin LLM wrappers over a notes table, high liability. Untouched: openFDA’s 1.28M adverse-event corpus, weekly recall feed, and municipal shelter lifecycle data. PawShield & Adopt-Fit own the “public-data-graph” lane and sidestep clinical-advice liability.

🧠 Mental health — single-modality middle

Mood loggers (Daylio, How We Feel), AI journals (Reflectly, Stoic, Rosebud), content libraries (Calm, Headspace). They reframe the present entry; almost none treat your history as a queryable, embedded corpus. Echo owns “retrieval-over-your-own-life.”

🎯 What these judges reward

The panel is AWS database specialists. Across every domain, the winning move is the same: make the JOIN between text-embeddings and structured signals the product — relational + time-series + pgvector + analytics in one Aurora instance — not a chatbot. All four concepts are built to do exactly that.

How to choose

Pick…

PawShield — if you want the safest high-ceiling outcome: cleanest data licensing, lowest build risk, an untouched dataset, and a demo that lands instantly. The recommendation.
Rhyme — if markets are your passion and you want the most impressive data-model demo, and you’ll commit to disciplined transcript-caching on day one.
Echo — if you care about mental health and a product whose value compounds with the user; accept the higher non-clinical-framing/privacy bar.
Adopt-Fit — if you want the most feel-good, lowest-liability build and the prettiest match-report UI, and you’re OK with a softer pure-B2C monetization story.
