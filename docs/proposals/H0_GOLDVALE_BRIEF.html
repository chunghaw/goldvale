Product Brief · Goldvale v2 · senior care + home rehab

Goldvale — the home-rehab companion for senior & chronic-care pets.

Goldvale was a calm daily companion for owners of senior and chronically-ill pets; it now becomes the owner-side home-rehabilitation companion for the single biggest senior/chronic burden — mobility decline (arthritis, post-op, neuro). The reframe: mobility is not a separate "rehab app" bolted onto the QoL check-in — it is woven through every existing surface. The 20-second daily check-in now captures a mobility sub-score and exercise tolerance; the symptom journal's pgvector recall now answers "did this stiffness flare resemble a past one, and what did we do last time?"; medication-adherence becomes exercise-and-modality adherence on the same engine; and the vet-prep brief now carries validated mobility-scale trends and adherence data your vet actually wants. Crucially, Goldvale sits in a real market gap: the existing rehab platforms (Wibbi, GoGoCharlie, Canine Exercise Solutions) are clinician-facing prescribing tools that assume a paying patient already has a credentialed rehab vet — a genuinely scarce professional — while wearables (PetPace, FitBark) give passive data with no guidance and demand hardware plus lock-in subscriptions. Goldvale is hardware-free, owner-facing, and explicitly non-clinical: it supports your vet's or rehab vet's multimodal plan (per the 2022 WSAVA and AAHA Pain Management Guidelines), tracks whether the prescribed work actually happened and how the animal tolerated it, surfaces pattern memory across the daily loop, and packages it all into a vet-visit brief — it never prescribes, diagnoses, grades lameness, or stages disease. It owns the bottom three tiers of the Physiotherapy Pyramid (environmental modification, exercise planning, home exercises) and hands the narrow clinic-only top (hydrotherapy, laser, shockwave, acupuncture) off to a professional via referral directories.

The product

Six features, woven through the daily loop

Each maps to a real Aurora data-model layer (relational/time-series/analytics), a pgvector use, and a Bedrock Claude role — with a hard non-clinical guardrail.

F1Mobility sub-score on the daily check-in (the wedge feature)

Extends the existing 20-second Villalobos HHHHHMM check-in with a tiny rotating mobility module: 2-3 plain-language observational items per day (e.g. 'limping noticed today? y/n', 'reluctant on stairs?', 'slipped on the floor?', 'rose easily after rest?'), drawn from a validated owner-reported mobility instrument. Periodically (weekly/monthly, vet-configurable) it administers a full validated scale to produce one numeric mobility score that trends over time. It computes change against the animal's OWN baseline and flags movements that cross a published MCID threshold (e.g. a 4-point LOAD drop, ~14-point Canine Orthopedic Index change) as 'a meaningful change worth mentioning to your vet' — never as a diagnosis.

Why it matters
Turns the vague worry 'is my old dog getting worse?' into a single trended number the owner and vet both trust, captured in the same low-friction loop they already do daily. Costs the owner near-zero extra effort because it piggybacks the existing check-in.
Daily-loop fit
This IS the daily loop — the mobility items are appended to the existing HHHHHMM check-in rather than living in a separate screen. The full-scale administration reuses the same check-in cadence engine. The resulting score becomes a first-class dimension alongside QoL, sitting next to medication adherence and the symptom journal.
Aurora model
Relational: pet, owner, scale_instrument (which CMI is active, licensing tier), scale_item, daily_checkin. Time-series: mobility_score_event (hypertable-style append-only, one row per scored administration: pet_id, instrument_id, raw_subscores JSONB, total_score, ts) plus daily_observation_event for the lightweight daily items. Analytics: window functions and a continuous rolling-baseline materialized view computing per-pet mean/stddev and MCID-crossing flags; trend slope over 30/90 days.
pgvector
Each completed scale administration is embedded (the item-response vector plus a short Claude-generated narrative of that period) so the journal can answer 'this mobility pattern resembles 5 weeks ago' by nearest-neighbour over the score-event embeddings, not just free-text notes.
Bedrock Claude
Bedrock Claude turns the raw subscores + delta into one calm sentence ('Mobility has drifted down about as much over the last month as it did before last autumn's flare — worth raising at the next visit'), and structures the MCID flag into plain language. Claude never computes the clinical score itself (deterministic scoring code does) and never stages or diagnoses.
Non-clinical guardrail: Frames every output as 'trend vs. your pet's own baseline' and 'a topic to bring to your vet' — never a diagnosis, severity grade, or treatment recommendation. Goldvale supports the vet's plan; the vet selects/administers the clinical instrument and interprets it. Non-embeddable scales (LOAD, CBPI, FMPI) are stored as vet-supplied results, not reproduced.  ·  Source: GenPup-M (PLOS ONE 2023, PMC10752556) — 24-item owner-reported canine MOBILITY instrument published CC-BY with the full questionnaire as supplementary file S2; legally embeddable/adaptable in-app with attribution. HCPI (Helsinki Chronic Pain Index, 11 items, freely available) as the low-friction default. LOAD/CBPI surfaced as vet-administered instruments the owner records (not redistributed). FMPI/FMPI-sf for cats (NC State, free non-commercial after permission form).
F2Vet-plan-gated home exercise track with safe progression logic

An owner imports (or a co-caregiver vet enters) a home exercise plan — sit-to-stand, cookie/lateral-flexion stretches, weight shifts, three-leg stands, cavaletti rails, controlled leash walks, balance-pad work. Goldvale schedules these into the daily loop with short video/illustrated 'how-to' cards from open rehab references and tracks completion + tolerance. It applies the transferable rehab progression framework — advance only ONE variable at a time (Frequency / Intensity / Time / Type), strength work 3-5x/week on alternate days, stretch/balance daily, sets of ~5 reps building toward 10 good-form reps before progressing, ~2 weeks before fitness gains then ~4-week increments — as a NUDGE engine: it suggests 'you've hit 10 clean reps for two weeks; consider asking your vet about progressing,' never auto-advancing. Exercise content is gated behind an existing vet plan; with no plan, the owner sees only environmental-modification and general-wellness content plus a referral prompt.

Why it matters
Directly attacks the ~50% home-exercise non-adherence problem (forgetting, vague guidance, too-hard programs) with reminders, clear visuals, and gentle right-sized progression — converting a static prescribed plan into a living adherence + tolerance feedback loop.
Daily-loop fit
Reuses the exact medication-adherence engine: today's exercises appear as check-off items in the daily loop alongside meds, with a one-tap tolerance rating ('handled it / a bit tired / sore / refused'). Fatigue signs (panting, lagging, limping, tremors, refusal) logged here flow into the same symptom journal.
Aurora model
Relational: exercise (controlled vocabulary), exercise_plan (vet-attributed, with credential tag CCRT/CCRP/DACVSMR), plan_item (target FITT params, target reps/sets), modality_modification (env mods, cryo/heat). Time-series: exercise_session_event (pet_id, exercise_id, planned vs completed reps, tolerance_rating, fatigue_flags JSONB, ts). Analytics: adherence-rate rollups per exercise and per week, tolerance-trend detection, and a progression-readiness view that fires the 'consider asking your vet to progress' nudge against the FITT rules.
pgvector
Tolerance/fatigue episodes are embedded so the journal can match 'this is how she reacted the last time we pushed the leash walks too fast' — surfacing the prior episode and what de-escalation followed.
Bedrock Claude
Bedrock Claude (1) generates the calm reminder and the progression-nudge copy from deterministic FITT/adherence signals; (2) writes the plain-language fatigue-debrief ('She refused the third set today — that matches a pattern of doing less on damp mornings'). Claude never invents exercises, never sets the dose, and never tells the owner to progress on its own authority — it relays the rule-engine suggestion as a question for the vet.
Non-clinical guardrail: Home-exercise content is GATED behind an existing vet/rehab-vet plan and labelled 'supports your vet's plan.' Goldvale never prescribes exercises, never sets or increases the dose, and stops/flags at fatigue signs. Progression is always framed as 'a question to ask your vet,' and the active-over-passive evidence is honoured by nudging exercise completion rather than passive-modality logging.  ·  Source: Open rehab references for the exercise vocabulary and how-to specs: AKC Canine Cavaletti at-home guide; CARE (Canine Arthritis Resources & Education) 'Therapeutic Exercise: 7 Key Principles'; Frontiers Vet Sci 2023 Physiotherapy Pyramid (PMC9863568); JFMS 2025 feline rehab review (PMC12227867) for cat-safe exercises. FITT progression framework from the same open sources.
F3Form-coaching pose check (explicitly non-diagnostic)

The owner optionally records a short phone video of an exercise (e.g. sit-to-stand or a leash walk pass). On-device/edge computer-vision pose estimation extracts consistency cues — is the rep complete, is form steady, is cadence even between sessions — and gives FORM-COACHING feedback ('try luring the nose a touch higher for a fuller stretch'; 'today's reps look as steady as last week's'). It tracks self-consistency over time, NOT clinical gait parameters. It never outputs joint angles as clinical measures, never grades lameness, never diagnoses.

Why it matters
Solves the 'vague guidance / poor visuals lead to errors' driver of non-adherence — the owner gets the kind of in-the-moment form correction a rehab tech would give over a telerehab call, building confidence they're doing it right.
Daily-loop fit
Hangs directly off the exercise track: a 'film this rep' option on any exercise card. The consistency score feeds the same exercise_session_event so the daily loop shows 'form steady' alongside completion and tolerance.
Aurora model
Relational: exercise_video (pet_id, exercise_id, storage ref, consent flag). Time-series: pose_consistency_event (per-session self-similarity score, completeness, cadence variance, ts). Analytics: per-exercise consistency trend; correlation view between consistency dips and tolerance/fatigue flags (for the owner's awareness only, never a clinical claim).
pgvector
Pose-feature embeddings per session enable 'this movement looks like her good weeks' self-similarity matching — nearest-neighbour over the pet's own historical movement embeddings, framed as consistency, not assessment.
Bedrock Claude
Bedrock Claude translates the CV consistency signals into warm, non-clinical coaching language and explicitly caveats it ('this is form-coaching, not a health assessment — for any concern about how she's moving, ask your vet'). Vision/pose runs in a CV pipeline; Claude only narrates.
Non-clinical guardrail: Hard non-clinical boundary: per the 2026 Frontiers review there is no validated home dog-gait system, so this is framed strictly as form-coaching and consistency-tracking — never gait analysis, lameness grading, or diagnosis. Goldvale keeps gait grading vet-side and routes any movement concern to the vet.  ·  Source: Open pose/gait research for the model substrate and honest framing: DeepLabCut, ViTPose++, YOLO-Pose (open); 2026 Frontiers review on canine gait CV (10.3389/fvets.2026.1729697) which establishes that NO validated smartphone dog-gait system exists and monocular error (~50mm) exceeds clinical thresholds. Open reference datasets: Dryad canine accelerometer set (doi:10.5061/dryad.m0cfxppbs), PLOS Figshare 1,259-stride dataset.
F4Condition-specific home-protocol templates (post-op TPLO, IVDD/neuro)

When a vet sets a recognised post-op or neuro recovery course, Goldvale instantiates a multi-week structured TRACK that paces the well-documented home arc day by day. Post-TPLO example: wk 0-2 strict confinement + 5-min leash walks 3-5x/day + PROM 'bicycle' 10 reps 3x/day + ice 15-20 min first 3-5 days, switching ice->heat after 3-5 days; graded leash-walk increase (~5->20 min by wk 8); weight-shifts and sit-to-stand added once weight-bearing returns ~wk 3-4; 8-week radiograph milestone gating off-leash. IVDD/neuro: sling-supported standing, weight-shifting, twice-daily limb massage, PROM, proprioception. Each template carries explicit RED FLAGS (post-op: sudden lameness after improving, swelling/heat, incision discharge/odor, knee clicking; neuro: sudden neuro worsening, new/complete loss of deep pain, acute inability to urinate) that surface as escalate-to-vet prompts.

Why it matters
The highest-anxiety, highest-stakes window for an owner is post-op or acute neuro recovery; Goldvale turns a confusing discharge sheet into a calm day-by-day track with milestones and clear 'call your vet now' triggers — the exact structured journey owners search for.
Daily-loop fit
The template populates the daily loop's exercise/modality items per recovery week automatically, so the daily check-in becomes the recovery tracker. Red-flag observations entered in the daily loop or journal trigger immediate escalation prompts.
Aurora model
Relational: protocol_template, protocol_phase (week-band, target activities, milestones), protocol_instance (pet_id, surgery/onset date, current phase), red_flag_rule. Time-series: protocol_progress_event and red_flag_observation_event (ts, severity, escalated y/n). Analytics: phase-on-schedule vs behind view; milestone-gating (won't surface 'off-leash ok?' question until the 8-week radiograph milestone is logged); red-flag frequency monitoring.
pgvector
Embeds each recovery period so a second surgery or the other limb can recall 'recovery is tracking like the left TPLO last year around week 4' — pattern memory across the animal's own recovery history.
Bedrock Claude
Bedrock Claude narrates 'where you are in recovery' in plain language, explains the next milestone, and — critically — phrases red-flag detections as urgent but non-diagnostic escalation ('these signs need a vet today; here's your vet's contact / the AARV directory'). Claude never confirms a complication; it routes.
Non-clinical guardrail: Templates are vet-initiated and labelled 'supports your surgeon's/vet's recovery plan'; Goldvale never starts a post-op protocol on its own. Red flags trigger 'contact your vet' escalation, never a diagnosis of the complication. Milestone gates (e.g. off-leash) defer to vet/radiograph confirmation.  ·  Source: Open TPLO/cruciate home protocols (TPLOInfo, GaitGuard week-by-week, dvm360 cranial-cruciate rehab); IVDD/neuro owner guidance (The Rehab Vet, KG Vet Physio) with red-flag lists; WSAVA/AAHA pain guidelines for the multimodal framing.
F5Environmental-modification home audit + habit loop (Pyramid base tier)

A short, room-by-room home audit (non-slip rugs/yoga mats, ramps and steps instead of jumping, raised food/water bowls, orthopedic bedding, clear traction paths) generates a personalised modification checklist — the evidence-based base of the Physiotherapy Pyramid and a first-line non-pharmacological pillar in WSAVA/AAHA. Completed mods are tracked, and the daily loop occasionally asks a habit-reinforcing question ('still using the ramp for the sofa?'). Available to ALL users with no vet plan required (it's environmental, not exercise), making it the safe on-ramp.

Why it matters
Gives every owner — even those with no rehab vet and no formal plan — an immediate, free, high-impact action that demonstrably reduces pain and fall risk, which builds trust and engagement before any exercise gating.
Daily-loop fit
The audit is a one-time setup; thereafter modification-adherence reminders ride the same daily-loop nudge engine as meds and exercises, and slips/falls logged in the journal feed back to suggest a missing modification.
Aurora model
Relational: home_zone, modification_type, pet_modification (status: suggested/done/declined, with reason). Time-series: modification_adherence_event and fall_slip_event. Analytics: a view correlating logged slip/fall events with un-actioned modifications to drive 'a non-slip mat here might help' suggestions; modification-completion score as an engagement and safety metric.
pgvector
Slip/fall and 'struggled to get up' journal entries are embedded so Goldvale can surface 'falls cluster on the kitchen tile — similar to entries before you added the hallway runner' pattern memory linking environment to incidents.
Bedrock Claude
Bedrock Claude personalises the audit results into a friendly prioritised checklist and explains WHY each mod helps (citing the guideline framing), and connects logged incidents to suggested mods. Non-clinical throughout.
Non-clinical guardrail: Purely supportive environmental guidance grounded in open guidelines; the only fully un-gated tier because it carries no exercise/injury risk. Still framed as 'supports your vet's plan' and 'general comfort and safety,' never as treatment of a diagnosed condition.  ·  Source: Physiotherapy Pyramid review (PMC9863568); 2022 WSAVA Global Pain Guidelines and 2022 AAHA Pain Management Guidelines (free PDFs) for the environmental-modification-as-first-line evidence; JFMS 2025 feline review for cat-specific mods (ramps, soft bedding, litter-box access).
F6Rehab-aware vet-prep brief + telerehab handoff and referral

Upgrades the existing vet-visit prep brief to lead with mobility: the validated-scale trend (with MCID-crossing flags), exercise adherence and tolerance summary, modality/modification adherence, logged red flags, BCS/MCS slow-trends, and the pgvector 'resembles a past flare' analogues — all grounded in cited open veterinary literature. It also surfaces a referral surface: when the owner has no rehab provider or the scale trend crosses a threshold, it offers the AARV / UT-CCRP / Canine Rehab Institute / ACVSMR public directories, and packages the brief into a format suited to a telerehab video consult (which the evidence shows is as satisfying as in-person).

Why it matters
Owners walk into the vet (or a telerehab call) with exactly the structured mobility data the vet needs to adjust the multimodal plan, instead of 'he seems a bit slower lately.' For the large majority with no nearby rehab vet, it routes them to one — owning the referral handoff without ever prescribing.
Daily-loop fit
The brief is the natural OUTPUT of everything the daily loop captured — scores, adherence, tolerance, red flags, modifications — assembled on demand before a visit. It reuses the existing co-caregiver sharing so a remote rehab vet can be granted view access.
Aurora model
Relational: vet_visit, rehab_provider, referral_event, literature_citation. Time-series: pulls the mobility_score_event, exercise_session_event, modification_adherence_event, red_flag_observation_event series into the brief window. Analytics: the brief is a parameterised aggregation across all time-series tables (trend slopes, adherence %, MCID flags, BCS/MCS deltas) materialised into one shareable document.
pgvector
Two ways: (1) semantic literature retrieval — embeds open canine-rehab/OA abstracts (from OpenAlex + PubMed E-utilities) so the brief cites the most relevant evidence for THIS pet's pattern; (2) the 'resembles N weeks ago' analogue search over the pet's own score/journal embeddings is included as context for the vet.
Bedrock Claude
Bedrock Claude is the brief author: it synthesises the time-series rollups, the pgvector analogues, and the retrieved citations into a calm, non-diagnostic, vet-ready summary ('Questions to discuss with your vet'), and drafts the telerehab-consult talking points. Claude is grounded and instructed never to diagnose, stage, or recommend treatment — only to summarise and pose questions.
Non-clinical guardrail: The brief is explicitly 'information to support your conversation with your vet,' phrased as questions and trends, never conclusions. Referral is to credentialed professionals (CCRT/CCRP/DACVSMR) — Goldvale routes OUT to keep itself non-clinical and never positions itself as the prescriber or the rehab provider.  ·  Source: OpenAlex API (250M+ works, no key, ~CC0 metadata) and NCBI PubMed E-utilities (free, no key) for the cited open literature; WSAVA 2022 and AAHA 2022/2023 guidelines as the backbone; AARV 'Find a Rehabilitation Professional', UT CCRP directory, Canine Rehab Institute CCRT finder, ACVSMR 'Find a Diplomate' as the public referral surfaces; telerehab evidence (PMC10286140).

The data model the judges score

One Aurora instance, four load-bearing layers

Built to genuinely exercise relational + time-series + pgvector + analytics in one Aurora PostgreSQL instance.

RELATIONAL (core entities + plans):

  • pet (species, dob, senior/chronic flags), owner, co_caregiver (sharing/permissions), vet, rehab_provider (with credential tag CCRT/CCRP/DACVSMR + AARV/directory link).
  • scale_instrument (which CMI, licensing tier: embeddable/vet-administered/gated), scale_item, exercise (controlled vocabulary + open-reference how-to card), modification_type, home_zone.
  • exercise_plan + plan_item (vet-attributed, FITT targets, target reps/sets), protocol_template + protocol_phase + protocol_instance (post-op/neuro), red_flag_rule, modality_modification.
  • vet_visit, referral_event, literature_citation.

TIME-SERIES (append-only event hypertables, the spine of trending):

  • daily_checkin / daily_observation_event (the existing HHHHHMM loop + lightweight mobility items).
  • mobility_score_event (per scale administration: instrument_id, raw_subscores JSONB, total_score, ts).
  • exercise_session_event (planned vs completed reps, tolerance_rating, fatigue_flags JSONB, ts).
  • pose_consistency_event (self-similarity, completeness, cadence variance).
  • modification_adherence_event, fall_slip_event.
  • medication_event (existing, now alongside exercise/modality adherence on one engine).
  • protocol_progress_event, red_flag_observation_event (escalated y/n).
  • bcs_event, mcs_event (slow monthly trends). Partition by time + pet_id; these are the columns the hackathon judges see as 'real time-series.'

PGVECTOR (semantic memory, several distinct uses):

  • journal_entry.embedding (existing symptom journal).
  • mobility_score_event.embedding — item-response + Claude period-narrative vectors for 'this mobility pattern resembles 5 weeks ago.'
  • tolerance_episode.embedding — exercise tolerance/fatigue episodes for 'how she reacted last time we pushed too fast.'
  • pose_feature.embedding — per-session movement self-similarity ('looks like her good weeks').
  • literature_chunk.embedding — open canine-rehab/OA abstracts (OpenAlex + PubMed) for grounded, cited vet-prep briefs. HNSW/IVFFlat indexes on each; cosine distance.

ANALYTICS (materialised views + window functions = the 'so what'):

  • rolling_baseline_mv: per-pet mean/stddev of each mobility scale + MCID-crossing flags (LOAD -4, COI -14, etc.).
  • adherence_rollup_mv: exercise/med/modification adherence % per week, tolerance-trend detection.
  • progression_readiness_mv: applies FITT rules to fire 'consider asking your vet to progress.'
  • protocol_schedule_mv: phase on-schedule vs behind, milestone gating.
  • incident_correlation_mv: links fall/slip events to un-actioned modifications.
  • vet_brief aggregation: parameterised cross-series rollup (trend slopes, adherence, MCID flags, BCS/MCS deltas, pgvector analogues, citations) materialised into one shareable document. This single-database design — relational integrity, time-series at scale, vector recall, and analytic rollups feeding a Bedrock-narrated brief — is the impressive Aurora story for AWS-database judges.
Home-exercise safety

Gated & guarded — never generative

Guidance is GATED and GUARDED, not generative. (1) Gating: home-exercise content only unlocks behind an existing vet/rehab-vet plan that the owner imports or a co-caregiver vet enters (with a credential tag — CCRT/CCRP/DACVSMR — recorded). With no plan, the owner sees only environmental-modification content (the safe Pyramid base) plus a referral prompt to the AARV/CCRP/ACVSMR directories. Goldvale never authors an exercise plan from scratch. (2) Controlled vocabulary: exercises come from a fixed, evidence-documented library (sit-to-stand, cookie/lateral-flexion stretches with the full nose-to-shoulder/sky/chest/floor/hip set, weight shifts, three-leg stands, cavaletti rails at documented heights/spacing, controlled leash walks on varied/inclined terrain, balance-pad work, PROM where instructed), each with an open-reference how-to card (video/illustration) so the owner has CLEAR VISUALS — directly targeting the 'vague guidance/poor visuals' driver of ~50% non-adherence. (3) Tracking: each scheduled exercise appears as a daily-loop check-off with a one-tap tolerance rating and fatigue-flag capture (panting, lagging, limping, tremors, refusal), logged to the exercise_session_event time-series and surfaced in the journal. (4) Safe progression as a NUDGE, never an action: a deterministic rule engine encodes the transferable framework — advance only ONE variable at a time (Frequency / Intensity / Time / Type, plus environment/impact); strength 3-5x/week on alternate days; stretch and balance daily; sets of ~5 reps building toward 10 'good-form' reps before progressing; expect ~2 weeks before fitness gains, then ~4-week increments; STOP at fatigue signs. When readiness criteria are met, Goldvale surfaces 'you've hit clean reps consistently — consider asking your vet about progressing,' phrased as a question for the vet, and NEVER auto-advances dose or intensity. The evidence that 'active exercises are definitely preferred over passive techniques' is honoured by nudging active-exercise completion and de-emphasising passive-modality logging. (5) Form support: the optional pose-check gives consistency-based form coaching, explicitly non-clinical. (6) Escalation: fatigue/red-flag signals route to the vet; Goldvale guides and tracks, the vet directs and doses.

Why it wins

The market gap it owns

Goldvale owns the space BETWEEN the two existing camps, for the cohort neither serves. Camp 1 — vet-prescribed HEP platforms (Wibbi with 2,500+ vets, GoGoCharlie with 500+ practices, Canine Exercise Solutions, Canine Rehab On Demand) — are clinician-FACING prescribing SaaS: a credentialed vet/CCRT builds a plan from a video library and pushes it with basic compliance reminders. They assume a paying rehab patient who ALREADY has a credentialed rehab therapist in the picture — and that therapist is genuinely scarce (CCRT/CCRP require postgrad coursework + supervised internship; AARV's directory is real but coverage is thin, so most owners have no nearby provider). They also have NO AI/pose feedback and, critically, do not own the daily-life loop. Camp 2 — wearables (PetPace, FitBark; note Whistle shut down) — give passive activity/posture/HRV data but no exercise guidance, no QoL narrative, and require a hardware purchase plus a lock-in subscription (PetPace ~$150 collar + ~$14-25/mo, 12-month commitment); PetPace's own peer-reviewed study admits its algorithms are proprietary/unvalidated (Actical R^2 only 0.56), leaving a trust/interpretation gap. The unserved cohort: owners managing a senior/chronic pet's mobility AT HOME who lack BOTH a nearby rehab vet AND a reason to buy hardware, and who churn on home programs (~50% non-adherence driven by forgetting and poor visuals). Goldvale's wedge is the hardware-free, owner-side, non-clinical daily COMPANION that (a) tracks whether prescribed exercises actually happened and how the animal tolerated them, (b) trends a validated owner-completed mobility scale, (c) surfaces 'this resembles a past flare' pattern memory via pgvector, and (d) packages it into a literature-cited vet-prep brief — supporting a vet's plan (or routing to a vet they don't yet have) rather than competing to be the prescriber. No incumbent folds rehab into an existing daily QoL + medication + semantic-symptom-journal loop with cross-source pattern memory. The cat side (feline DJD is 30-90% radiographic, up to 45% with clinical signs, and badly under-served) is a further open differentiator.

Risks & guardrails

What could go wrong (and the mitigation)

Non-clinical line creep — the gravest risk. Mobility scores, MCID flags, pose data, and condition templates all FEEL diagnostic; a single piece of copy that says 'Bramble has worsening arthritis' or auto-computes a COAST stage or lameness grade breaches the non-clinical stance and creates liability. Mitigation: deterministic scoring (never Claude), Claude prompts hard-constrained to 'trends + questions for your vet,' gait grading and COAST staging kept strictly vet-side, every surface labelled 'supports your vet's plan.'
Pose CV over-promising — the 2026 Frontiers review is explicit that no validated home dog-gait system exists and monocular error (~50mm) exceeds clinical thresholds; if marketed as 'gait analysis' it's both wrong and a liability. Mitigation: strictly form-coaching/consistency framing with an explicit in-app caveat.
Scale licensing — embedding LOAD/CBPI/FMPI/COAST verbatim without permission infringes Elanco/UPenn/NC State copyright. Mitigation: embed only CC-BY GenPup-M and freely-available HCPI; surface the rest as vet-administered (store results, don't redistribute); secure the FMPI non-commercial/commercial license before any cat-paid feature.
Exercise-injury liability — guiding unsupervised home exercise can cause harm (AKC: 'misuse of underwater treadmills can cause further injury'; over-hard programs are a known non-adherence/harm driver). Mitigation: gate all exercise behind a vet plan, never auto-progress dose, stop at fatigue signs, keep clinic-only modalities out of owner hands.
Red-flag false reassurance/alarm — missing or over-firing post-op/neuro red flags (loss of deep pain, can't urinate) is dangerous either way. Mitigation: red flags route to 'contact your vet now,' never to a Goldvale judgment; conservative thresholds.
Adherence/engagement risk — the whole thesis rests on the daily loop beating ~50% non-adherence; if the rehab additions make the 20-second check-in feel heavy, engagement drops. Mitigation: rotate only 2-3 mobility items per day, piggyback existing flows, never add a separate rehab screen.
Competitive/positioning risk — incumbents (Wibbi, GoGoCharlie) could add an owner daily loop; PetPace owns wearable mindshare. Mitigation: lean into the integrated QoL+med+journal+pattern-memory moat and the hardware-free, no-rehab-vet-required wedge, and ship cat coverage where incumbents are weak.
Data/privacy — pet video and health data require clear consent and storage controls (consent flag on every exercise_video); avoid any real personal-clinical data in the public demo path.
